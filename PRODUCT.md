# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Self-coached lifters who want a clear, dependable way to log training without relying on a coach-facing platform or an overbuilt fitness system. They are often moving through a workout in real time and need the product to stay out of the way while still preserving a trustworthy training record.

## Product Purpose
LiftLog is an adaptive training companion and personal lifting journal for self-coached lifters. It turns a lifter's goals, schedule, training location, equipment, experience, and muscle priorities into a practical plan, then uses the training log to give specific progression guidance. It helps each session stay easy to start, adjust, complete, and review without becoming a coach-facing platform or a wall of analytics.

## Brand Personality
Easy, encouraging, and disciplined. The product should feel calm and supportive, with enough structure to reinforce good training habits without sounding demanding or intense. It should help users feel capable and steady, never judged or pushed around.

## Anti-references
This should not feel overbearing, macho, or performative. Avoid aggressive gym culture cues, hyper-gamified habit pressure, and bloated dashboard energy. It should also avoid feeling clinical or cold in a way that makes training feel like data entry instead of a personal practice.

## Design Principles
1. Reduce friction at every step: opening the app, understanding the plan, logging a set, and finishing a session should all feel immediate.
2. Support independent training: the interface should respect the self-coached lifter, explain why a recommendation was made, and make every recommendation editable.
3. Keep the record trustworthy: progress, recovery from interruptions, and session history should feel dependable enough to become part of a real training habit.
4. Encourage without pressure: use clarity and warmth to support consistency, but never drift into guilt, hype, or unnecessary urgency.
5. Let simplicity carry the experience: advanced planning, progression, substitution, and history logic should surface as one clear next decision rather than extra dashboard weight.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA where practical. Maintain clear focus states, strong enough contrast for routine daily use, and reduced-motion-friendly behavior. The interface should stay readable, calm, and easy to operate on mobile, especially during active workout sessions when attention is split.
